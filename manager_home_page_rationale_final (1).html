Why we're building it, what problem it solves, and where it's headed.

The problem

Managers log in and don't know where to start

The Ausmed platform was built for org managers — the L&D coordinators who configure and oversee the system. Team managers, the facility and ward-level managers who chase compliance day-to-day, were given access to the same interface. Most of it doesn't apply to them.

As we've added Learn, Perform, Competency, and soon Events, the problem has compounded. More products means more places to check — not more clarity.

There is no single landing point. A manager logs in and arrives mid-flow — on the LMS dashboard, or wherever their last session left them — with no clear view of what needs their attention. More products means more places to check, not more clarity.

The compliance picture

Not evenly distributed — and that matters

Across 631 organisations with known compliance data, the mean learning compliance rate is 75%. But the distribution tells a more important story than the average.

36% of orgs are below 70%
133 organisations are under 50% compliant. 94 more are between 50–69%. These are the organisations most likely to have disoriented managers missing tasks or not logging in at all.
37% of orgs are at 100%
233 organisations have achieved full compliance. This proves the outcomes are achievable — the gap is not a product capability issue, it's a usability and clarity issue.

The distribution is bimodal. The mean of 75% understates the urgency — it's not that most orgs are nearly there, it's that a large group is failing while another is thriving. The home page has its most direct impact on the bottom third.

Root cause

Fragmentation — not missing features

Most of what managers need already exists in the platform. The problem is that it's split across disconnected surfaces, presented inconsistently, with no clear path to action.

No single starting point. Managers land mid-flow — on the LMS dashboard, or wherever their last session left them. No orientation to what matters across any product.
Split task lists. Perform tasks in one place, compliance follow-ups in another, competency assessments somewhere else. There is no unified view of what needs action today.
No team visibility. No single place to see who they manage and what those people need — especially as new products add more task types to the mix.
Eroded trust in data. Inconsistent metrics, broken displays, and duplicate dashboards mean managers stop acting when they can't trust what they're seeing.
What we're building

Six targeted changes. One surface.

Not a platform redesign — a stable landing point that makes the existing platform coherent for every manager, from day one.

One place to land. Managers arrive at a home page, not mid-flow in a product dashboard. A consistent starting point every time they log in.
Unified task list. Everything that needs action — from Learn, Perform, and Competency — in one view. No more visiting each product separately.
Team snapshot. Who they manage, current compliance rate, who needs a nudge. The information managers actually need, without digging for it.
Quick actions. One-click access to the most common tasks. Built for a time-poor environment — aged care managers don't have time to navigate.
A compliance number they trust. Consistent, clearly defined, surfaced the same way across every context. No more confusion between completion and compliance rates.
Instrumented to learn. We track what managers engage with and how usage correlates with compliance improvement. This feeds directly into what comes next.
Where this is headed

The home page is the foundation, not the destination

What we learn from these six changes will directly inform the broader Compliance Hub concept — an intuitive place for managers to understand and manage compliance across all products — being explored in parallel by the wider team.
